Nature's Bystanders

Nature’s Bystanders was an interactive installation designed as an exploration of trees as passive observers to societal change.

My Role

Lead of User Experience + Lead of Technology Architecture & Development

Team

Henrique Salvador, Larissa Waterman, Blair Yang, Curtis Neuman, Emilli Wright & Ryan James

Type & Duration

Team Project - 2 months

Process

01. Brief

In this assignment we were asked to look back and explore our relationship with nature. To design a future that is more tactile, phydital and human, we explored the relationship between nature and societal & technological change.

For the process, we dived into Google’s efficient Design Sprint, aiming to quickly answer the brief’s question, ideate, prototype and test.

"Back to reality — Questioning the future of our relationship with nature."

02. Understand

In this phase, we explored a series of case studies that successfully bridged the gap between natural materials and technology.


Our biggest inspiration was the installation Matières Sensibles, by Grégory Lasserre & Anaïs met den Ancxt, a series of sculptures made of veneer sheets of wood, which have sonorous touch zones that follow the veins of the wood — this allows the sculpture to produce sound when touched. The designers at Scenocosme are creating these interactive pieces to hybridize natural elements and technology through creating symbolic and sensorial relationships between the body and the natural environment. Thus, producing a sensitive, emotion based relationship between the living and the natural elements, which are often viewed as “invisible” or “without feeling” through the use of touch, sight and sound.


Based on the precedents analyzed, we tried to map out emotions that people interacting with such installations felt:

  • confusion through trying to figure out how the sounds correlate with the touch;
  • satisfaction through their unique composition of sounds;
  • wonder and respect for the natural component;
  • peace from the spectrum of frequencies given by the piece;
  • concentration when they try to identify where each sound lies on the piece.

03. Define

During the Define phase, we drew from the precedents, concepts and technologies and started brainstorming ways of storytelling "phyditally" — that is, by margin physical, natural materials with digital technology.


It was at this point, looking at what nature represents and how it relates to technology that we started having conversations about how the trees in our cities have been standing there as witnesses to the changes around them. We, finally, narrowed it down to designing an installation that looks at trees as bystanders to societal development.


04. Sketch

At the beginning of the Sketch phase, we had a clear "How Might We" that we wanted to move on with. With that constraint now in mind, we started ideating and sketching different ways we could tell that story via interactive installations.

How might we tell an interactive story about trees as bystanders to societal change?

05. Decide

With dozens of different ideas sketched out, we individually presented our concepts to the whole group and discussed different aspects of each. With the brief and goal in mind, we then ran a session of dot voting, where we achieved consensus on what our installation could become.

Chosen Concept

The concept we decided to go with was a three-walls immersive room with a tree stump in the middle. A single projected image spans through the three walls showing a specific point in time. By interacting with the tree stump, the user is able to change the image being projected — from the inner to the outer veins of the tree stump, the user is able to move from earliest to latest years — thus empathizing with the tree’s experience of witnessing the change in its environment through time.

We chose to tell the story of a tree in the Toronto Islands as a bystander to the the changes in the Toronto’s skyline from the 1880s to 2017.

06. Prototype - Step 1

At this point, we split into two groups. The first group was responsible for planning the physical measurements of the installation and making the wood.

07. Prototype - Step 2

The second group, which I led, was responsible for designing the tactile interaction and the hardware/software architecture behind the touch.
