Smart Cistern App

The Smart Cistern App helps users to remotely drain their cisterns, manage automated drainages and check stats about the collection of stormwater in their properties.

Smart Cistern App

DISCLAIMER: Due to an NDA, only a fraction of the research is being showcased here. The client name and the app logo were also purposely omitted.

My Role

UX Researcher and Designer

Goal

To redesign the Smart Cistern app MVP employing a user-centred approach – through interviews, task analysis, usability testing and heuristic evaluations of the existing design.

Type & Duration

Client Work - 2 months

Existing Design & Challenge

The existing design features two main screens: the first, an overview of the user's cistern; the second, charts about the technical components of the cistern and overall stormwater collection. There is also a hamburger menu where the user can select different addresses and navigate to secondary screens (Q&A and Options).

The challenge was to make the app, whose target audience is quite broad, delightful and easier to use while retaining only the features that are meaningful to the user's experience.

Heuristic Evaluation

The first step towards evaluating the current design was to run a Heuristic Analysis of the existing UI. For that, I followed Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics for User Interface Design and, where applicable, Xerox's Heuristic Evaluation Checklist.

Interviews & Task Analysis

At this stage, 3 interviews were done with potential and current users to try and identify:

  • What their main goals are.
  • What they enjoy about the current design.
  • What issues they currently face when using the app.
  • Missed opportunities that we could explore with the redesign.

At this point, I also wrote a very detailed analysis of their tasks and the necessary steps to complete them – aiming to identify possible issues, challenges and usability considerations.

Sketching

Having the findings from the user study and the heuristic analysis, it was time to start sketching ideas on how to improve the experience. Below, are some of the sketches from this phase.

High Fidelity Wireframes

After reviewing the sketches and low-fidelity wireframes with the client and potential users, a few adjustments were made and I dove into Sketch and Principle to design the high fidelity prototypes.

The proposed design focus on minimalistic features, keeping only the most important information necessary to execute the tasks. Some information was taken out and the information architecture was also changed based on research findings.


Animations

The animated transitions and UI feedback play a vital role in this prototype. They aim to improve the visibility of how the user's actions affect their physical cistern (e.g. how much water will be drained vs how much water will be left).

On top of that, they help users better locate themselves within the app architecture, in addition to improving the overall experience through delightful micro-interactions.
